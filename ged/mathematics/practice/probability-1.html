Probability Practice 1

Question 1

1. In drawing cards from a deck, any single card has an equal chance of being drawn. After six cards have been drawn and removed, what is the probability of drawing an ace of hearts if it has not yet been drawn?

Question 2

2. A standard deck of playing cards has 52 cards, with 13 cards each of hearts, diamonds, clubs, and spades. If a card is drawn randomly from the deck, what is the probability that it will be either hearts or diamonds?

Question 3

3. There are 15 colored chips in a bag. Eight are green, and seven are white. Five white chips are removed. What is the probability that the next chip selected will be green?

Question 4

4. Suppose you roll the die 66 times. How many times are you likely to roll a 6?

Question 5

5. If an outfit consists of one shirt, one pair of pants, and one pair of shoes, how many distinct outfits can Noah make?
